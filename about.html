The Boring Stuff

When it comes to writing about myself, I tend to struggle so I will try my best but as you can see from the Quick Stats, My name is Ryan Elliott, I'm 25 years old and currently have over 4 years coding experience. To find out what languages I have experience in, just keep reading and scrolling.

Playing The Sympathy Card

Due to personal issues at home and school, I left at 14 years old and had to go and live with my dad. However, he was not able to get me into another school which meant I was free to go along with him to work and not look back.

This of course had a huge impact on my future which at the time I didn't give much thought to. So as a result, I struggled to achieve the many things everyone else around me had or were achieving everyday. This unfortunately also had an impact on my mental health. I began to get depressed and started to wonder if I'd ever get anywhere and wished I could go back and fight through the problems I had in order to put me in a position to get to where I wanted to be.

Let Us Not Dwell On The Past

I could have gone on forever about how bad life choices have put me in the position I am in today but dwelling on the past will get you nowhere and will stop you from trying to improve your future.

At a certain point when I was 16, I realized that I really needed to sort my life out and ensure I have a bright future for not only myself but my future family.

This however wasn't very easy as getting a job and working for someone else was not on the agenda as I wanted to work for myself and not have someone else shouting down my ear. So I went to work and tried to think of a way I could make myself some money. After a year or so I thought of something. A Website. Yes I wanted to give it a go as gaining the knowledge needed to do such a thing was something I wanted growing up. So I started to look into how you create a website, which began with knowing what I'd need to learn about web development.

Not wanting to push myself too far, I decided HTML and CSS would be the way to go. So after a lot of research, help and experimenting, I made my very first website from pure HTML & CSS! But I had a problem, no money to actually publish the website so unfortunately it didn't make it online, however I achieved something I never thought I would.

Fast forward another year or so and after a few other failed attempts of making something of myself, I wanted to learn how to develop Android APPS. This was something I attempted a few times but I only got as far as downloading and installing Eclipse because as soon as I was about to start making my first app, I chickened out and gave up instantly. However I didn't give up for long because one day I said to myself "Right, I really want to give it a proper go!" so I came up with a game plan.

I knew that just downloading the needed software and just going into it was never going to work so I figured the best approach was to think of an app I wanted to make and just make it, no matter how hard it may be. So I did. By the end it meant I had managed to teach myself HTML, CSS and now Java.

I'm going to leave this section here now as what you've already read has hardly scratched the surface! So Providing I haven't bored you too much, please carry on reading through until the end of the page where you can find more information on my coding history and details of the many Android apps I have created along with my second Website.

What made me get started?

As far as I can remember I have adored technology, especially when it comes to the way it works but mainly when it comes to how websites and mobile apps are created! I used to think I'd need to be some scientific genius to create such things so for a long time I just appreciated the work that was put in and the knowledge the developers have.

Until One day I decided I wanted to give it a go, which if you've gotten this far, you'll know that I took the big step and started involving myself in the developing world. First with a Website, then with an Android app.
